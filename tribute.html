Ratan Tata

Facts

Born

28 December 1937 Bombay, Bombay Presidency, British India

Education

Ratan Tata completed his schooling at the Campion School in Mumbai. He then pursued a degree in architecture from Cornell University in the United States. Later, he attended the Harvard Business School and completed the Advanced Management Program.

Career at Tata Group

Ratan Tata joined the Tata Group in 1962 and started his career working on the shop floor of Tata Steel, the flagship company of the Tata Group. Over the years, he held various positions within the group, including managing director and chairman.

Chairman of Tata Group

Ratan Tata served as the chairman of Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group, from 1991 to 2012. Under his leadership, the group expanded globally and diversified its business interests into sectors such as automobiles, steel, telecommunications, information technology, hospitality, and more.

Honors and Recognitions

Ratan Tata has received numerous honors and recognitions throughout his career. He was awarded the Padma Bhushan in 2000 and the Padma Vibhushan in 2008, which are two of the highest civilian awards in India. He has also been recognized internationally, including being listed among the world's most influential people by Forbes and Time magazine.

Philanthropy

Ratan Tata is known for his philanthropic efforts. He has been actively involved in initiatives related to education, healthcare, and rural development. He established the Tata Trusts, which are among India's oldest and largest philanthropic organizations.
